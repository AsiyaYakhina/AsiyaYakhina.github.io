Concept Design Project

Innovating tourism in China with MIT Design Lab

My role: Exploring common problems faced by tourists in China, brainstorming with the team to find potential solutions, contributing to concept definition and development, designing journey maps, designing end-to-end tourist experiences, wireframing interfaces designs, and creating interactive high-fidelity mockups of the interfaces.

Research and brainstorming

The research team under the MIT Design Lab has investigated various problems experienced by tourists in Chinese cities, from high-traffic locations like Beijing to quieter scenic destinations like Anshun. Through innovative clustering and analysis of user-generated online travel reviews, researchers identified pain points in the Chinese tourism experience. In the brainstorming phase, we aimed to generate ideas that would improve tourists’ experiences using the technology they already have. For this reason, we chose to focus mobile as the main platform.

Eating

Problem: Popular restaurants in Beijing are often busy. For instance, Peking Duck Restaurant always has a line in front of it. Customers have to get there before 11am; after 11am there is a long wait to acquire a table.

Solution: A mobile restaurant reservation app that reduces crowds at restaurants while encouraging more in-depth engagement by tourists in the cities they’re visiting.
