Concept Design Project

Innovating tourism in China with MIT Design Lab

My role: exploring common problems faced by tourists in China, brainstorming solutions, contributing to concept definition and development, designing journey maps, designing end-to-end tourist experiences, wireframing interfaces designs, and creating interactive high-fidelity mockups of the interfaces.

Research and brainstorming

The research team under the MIT Design Lab has investigated various problems experienced by tourists in Chinese cities, from high-traffic locations like Beijing to quieter scenic destinations like Anshun. Through innovative clustering and analysis of user-generated online travel reviews, researchers identified pain points in the Chinese tourism experience. In the brainstorming phase, we aimed to generate ideas that would improve tourists’ experiences using the technology they already have. For this reason, we chose to focus mobile as the main platform.

Shopping: Problem

Most tourists in China, particularly from the Western countries, have a hard time shopping at local markets in smaller cities like Anshun and Xinxian. They cannot converse with the sellers because of the language barrier. Moreover, they lack the cultural knowledge of how to bargain - something that is extremely accepted and widely common in Asian shopping cultures.

Solution: bargaining assistant

A mobile app that teaches tourists how to bargain and then guides them through the process - ensuring a fun experience and a good deal.

Transportaion: Problem

Because public transportation in smaller Chinese cities is not easy to access, tourists are often forced to rely on private taxis. It is difficult to know whether a given car is safe to get in or if the driver has a good record.

Solution: Lyft for private taxis

A mobile app that combinines real-time location with user-generated review data in a mobile interface that makes it easy for users to find information about, select and review private taxi operators.

Eating: Problem

Popular restaurants in Beijing are often busy. For instance, Peking Duck Restaurant always has a line in front of it. Customers have to get there before 11am; after 11am there is a long wait to acquire a table.

Solution: reservation assistant

A mobile restaurant reservation app that reduces crowds at restaurants while encouraging more in-depth engagement by tourists in the cities they’re visiting.
