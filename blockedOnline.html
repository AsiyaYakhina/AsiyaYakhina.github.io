Blocked Online

Unveiling internet censorship

I worked on this independent project with Kiarash Adl under the supervision of Tim Berners-Lee.

We collaborated closely to develop the concept and the vision behind the project. My personal contributions included designing the website at www.blockedonline.com iterating from wireframes to pixel-perfect mockups, designing a system of icons and a logo, and working with the developer to ensure accurate design implementation.

Problem

There is little transparency about internet censorship in the world. Some resources can help the user tell if the website is blocked in their area, however it is difficult to tell whether a given website is blocked in another country. Stats about online censorhip around the world are also limited to numbers describing how "bad" the censorhip is, but lack depth and comprehensiveness.

Solution

BlockedOnline uses crowdsourcing approach to gather data on internet censorship. This project relies on people from various countries to download the application from the website, run it on their local machine and thereby provide information on blocked websites in their region. The goal behind Blocked Online is to provide accessible and readable information on internet censorship in different countries and to raise global awareness of what often remains hidden from the eyes of the public.

Website overview

The landing page presents the user with an interactive map showing statistics on blocked websites around the world, as well as two main action buttons inviting the user to either explore data or get involved in the project.

Under the “Explore Data” tab, the user can research censorship facts by URL or by country.

The following templates show search results by country and by website. The structure of the pages allows users to view information quickly, but dive into specific topics if they’re interested.

Under the “Get Involved” tab, the user can download the application, learn more about the project and find our contact information.
