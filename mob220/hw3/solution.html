Pendleton Hall

General information

Pendleton Hall is a long building on the north edge of Wellesley's academic quad. The building is home to many academic programs: Anthropology, Art, Economics, Education, Japanese, Political Science and Sociology.

History

Named for Wellesley's first alumna president, Ellen Pendleton 1886, this academic hall was built in 1934. The word Chemistry was engraved over its main west entry, and science still filled the classrooms in Pendleton West well into the 20th century.
